# Live News Social Audience Intelligence Brief

Last updated: 2026-05-08

## Purpose

Live News is building a private Social Publisher that turns source-linked Live News stories into Instagram and Facebook drafts. The goal is not to chase cheap engagement. The goal is to create accurate, useful, emotionally aware posts that move people from social platforms to exact Live News article pages.

This brief is the baseline knowledge layer for future social-publishing agents.

## Growth Structure To Preserve

The social system should not be one agent that writes a caption and walks away. It should work like a protected learning stack:

1. Source data enters from Live News only.
2. Audience Pattern Mapper identifies safe human-relevance patterns.
3. Caption Variant Agent creates several rhythm options.
4. Teacher layers check source safety, emotional fit, repetition, platform fit, growth memory, and human approval.
5. Human editor approves or rejects the private draft.
6. After posting manually or through approved API later, aggregate performance becomes Social Style Memory.
7. Social Style Memory improves future drafts without storing private people, copied comments, tokens, or personal information.

The system grows by learning which approved patterns help users reach exact Live News article pages. It does not grow by becoming more aggressive, more emotional, or more willing to guess.

## Teacher Layers

These layers are now the intended structure for social publishing work:

- Source Safety Teacher: blocks missing attribution, missing original source links, homepage-only links, and unsupported facts.
- Human Relevance Teacher: checks whether the story has a clear human angle such as residents, workers, consumers, fans, public safety, or global impact.
- Rhythm Variety Teacher: checks that caption variants do not all start the same way or sound like a template.
- Platform Fit Teacher: checks square card plans, source lines, readable titles, and platform-specific captions.
- Growth Memory Teacher: allows learning only from aggregate performance patterns such as link clicks, shares, saves, comments, reach, views, and hides.
- Human Approval Teacher: keeps everything private and review-only until the editor approves it.

This is the "code behind code" safety shape: every creative agent needs a teacher layer that can block, warn, or record a lesson.

## What The Platforms Reward

Instagram and Facebook ranking systems do not simply show posts in chronological order. They use signals to predict whether a person is likely to care, interact, watch, save, share, comment, or tap.

Important signals for Live News:

- Recentness matters, especially for news.
- Post type matters because some users respond more to video, photos, carousels, or links.
- Engagement matters, especially likes, comments, reactions, shares, saves, and watch behavior.
- Past relationship matters; people who interacted with the Page before are more likely to see future posts.
- Topic interest matters; users who watch or engage with a topic may see more related content.
- Integrity matters; misinformation, low-quality sensitive content, spam, or copied material can reduce reach or eligibility for recommendations.
- Variety matters; platforms avoid overloading people with too much of the same author or media type in a row.

## Audience Reality

People do not open Instagram or Facebook to read a newspaper. They open it for signals:

- What happened?
- Does this affect me or my family?
- Is this surprising, serious, useful, local, emotional, or worth sharing?
- Can I understand it in seconds?
- Do I trust the source?
- Is there a clear next step?

Live News posts should meet users where they are, then invite them to the full article.

## Emotional Intelligence Rules

Social captions should feel human without becoming manipulative.

Use:

- Clarity before cleverness.
- A calm tone for serious stories.
- Human impact when the feed data supports it.
- Specific stakes: residents, workers, families, consumers, fans, voters, travelers, students.
- Short sentences mixed with one context sentence.
- Curiosity based on facts, not clickbait.

Avoid:

- Fear bait.
- Fake urgency.
- “You won’t believe” language.
- Overheated outrage.
- Unsupported opinion.
- Copying publisher wording.
- Copying comments from users.
- Turning tragedy into entertainment.

## Comment Intelligence

Comments can help Live News learn, but they must be used safely.

Good uses:

- Detect confusion: “What does this mean?” “Where is this happening?”
- Detect local relevance: residents naming roads, schools, neighborhoods, events.
- Detect trust issues: users questioning source, image, headline, or missing context.
- Detect useful follow-up angles: repeated questions or unclear terms.
- Detect tone problems: users reacting negatively to sensational wording.

Bad uses:

- Copying user comments into captions.
- Treating one loud comment as majority opinion.
- Using comments as verified facts.
- Scraping private or personal information.
- Building posts to provoke fights.

The model should extract patterns, not people.

## Live News Social Post Shapes

Use different shapes so the account does not sound robotic.

### Breaking Clarity

Best for fast-moving top stories.

Format:

- What happened.
- What is known now.
- Source-linked read-more link.

### Local Impact

Best for city/local stories.

Format:

- Place first.
- Who is affected.
- What changes next if known.

### Human Stakes

Best for public-safety, health, sports, entertainment, and community stories.

Format:

- Person/group affected.
- Why people are reacting.
- Full source-linked coverage link.

### Explainer Hook

Best for policy, business, technology, courts, and complex national/international stories.

Format:

- Plain-language issue.
- One practical consequence.
- Full source-linked coverage link.

### Watchlist

Best for stories where full details are still developing.

Format:

- Known facts only.
- What remains unclear.
- “Live News is tracking source-linked coverage.”

## Caption Quality Gates

Before a post can be ready:

- It must point to an exact Live News article URL, not only the homepage.
- It must include or preserve original source attribution.
- It must avoid copied publisher wording.
- It must not add facts missing from Live News article data, feed metadata, or approved source research.
- It must avoid robotic framing.
- It must match the article category and seriousness.
- It must have a clear reader reason to click.
- It must be reviewed by a human until the system has proven quality over time.

## Metrics To Learn From

The social system should learn from aggregate performance, not personal data.

Track:

- Reach/views.
- Likes/reactions.
- Comments.
- Shares.
- Saves.
- Link clicks to exact Live News article pages.
- Click-through rate by post type.
- Caption style used.
- Story category.
- Posting time.
- Whether the post was image, carousel, reel, or link post.
- Whether comments showed confusion, correction, anger, usefulness, or local knowledge.

Do not optimize for likes alone. For Live News, the strongest score is:

`accurate post + useful engagement + exact article clicks + no safety flags`

## Social Style Memory

Social Style Memory should grow slowly and safely. It should store:

- Which caption shapes were approved.
- Which post categories brought exact article clicks.
- Which visual shapes helped users understand the story quickly.
- Which posting times performed well by category.
- Which repeated audience questions showed confusion.
- Which tone patterns caused hides, reports, or editor rejection.

It should not store:

- Instagram or Facebook access tokens.
- Private messages.
- Usernames or profile data.
- Copied comment wording.
- Claims from comments treated as facts.

The memory should teach future agents without exposing the public to unapproved output.

## Recommended First Build Direction

The next system should be a private feedback loop:

1. Generate multiple caption variants per story.
2. Keep all variants private.
3. Human approves one.
4. Post manually or through approved API later.
5. Record performance metrics.
6. Store aggregate lessons in a Social Style Memory.
7. Improve future drafts from verified performance patterns.

## Suggested Agent Roles

- Social Story Selector: chooses stories worth social promotion.
- Audience Fit Agent: decides whether the angle is local, emotional, practical, urgent, or explanatory.
- Caption Variant Agent: creates 3-5 caption options with different rhythms.
- Source Safety Agent: checks facts, links, attribution, and publisher-wording distance.
- Comment Pattern Agent: later reads only approved public engagement data and summarizes patterns.
- Performance Teacher Agent: updates social style memory from aggregate results.
- Human Approval Gate: blocks publishing until approved.

## Build Order

1. Keep the Social Publisher private and noindex.
2. Create exact Live News article pages for approved stories.
3. Generate caption variants and social card plans for exact article pages only.
4. Let the editor approve one variant.
5. Post manually first.
6. Record aggregate performance after posting.
7. Feed approved lessons into Social Style Memory.
8. Connect Meta posting only after manual quality is reliable and permissions are approved.

## Platform Notes

- Instagram Feed ranking predicts actions such as commenting, liking, saving, tapping, and watching; posts are scored and also subject to content-quality and diversity rules.
- Facebook Feed ranking weighs engagement, post type, engagement volume, and recentness.
- Meta recommendations try to avoid recommending low-quality, problematic, or sensitive content even when that content does not violate rules.
- Instagram Insights can show trends and performance for business/creator accounts, including views, reach, interactions, engaged accounts, follower growth, locations, ages, and active times.
- Reels should be high quality. Instagram guidance supports 30 FPS minimum and 720p minimum; boosted Reels commonly require vertical full-screen formatting and limits around 90 seconds depending placement.
