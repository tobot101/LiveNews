LIVE NEWS EXECUTION PLAN
Primary product plan for the website, article system, and front-page redesign
Prepared: 2026-04-23

PURPOSE
Live News should become a readability-first, source-respectful news destination that
turns fast-moving web coverage into cleaner, easier-to-browse story pages. The site
should not feel like a raw RSS wall. It should feel like a calm newsroom product that
surfaces the most important stories first, summarizes them clearly, and always points
readers to the original source.

LOCKED PRODUCT DECISIONS TO PRESERVE
Brand: Live News
Tagline: Anytime & Anywhere
No ads
No community hub
Day mode uses a cool blue-gray low-glare palette
Dark mode should be eye-friendly and readable
Top Stories remains capped at 8
Latest News Feed remains capped at 170 in the backend
User-facing feed controls should stay 30, 50, and 100
Stories should age out around 48 hours
Local News must stay near the top of the homepage
Local browsing should happen on the dedicated local page
Attribution and original source links are required
Live News must not copy publisher wording or article structure

WHY THE CURRENT HOMEPAGE NEEDS TO EVOLVE
The current homepage is useful, but most stories still read like feed rows. The title is
the main visible asset, and the source/time line does more work than the summary does.
That makes scanning harder than it should be, especially when many stories look similar.

The next version should turn story rows into structured cards and turn major stories into
real Live News pages. This does not mean hand-writing every article from scratch. It means
building a system that:
1. Ingests source material from the web.
2. Clusters matching reports.
3. Produces a simple Live News summary in original wording.
4. Publishes a dedicated Live News page for that story.
5. Links back to the original source and any supporting sources.

This is the right path because it gives Live News:
- indexable article URLs
- better SEO structure
- stronger internal linking
- stable destinations for social posts
- a cleaner experience than sending users directly away to the publisher first

PRODUCT DIRECTION FOR THE NEW HOMEPAGE
Live News should borrow the information hierarchy used by major U.S. news sites without
copying any single outlet's style. The key patterns worth using are:
- a lead story or lead package at the top
- visible categories and recency cues
- grouped modules rather than one long undifferentiated list
- clear summaries beneath or beside headlines
- strong source and timestamp context
- separate lanes for live updates, local coverage, and latest items

RECOMMENDED HOMEPAGE LAYOUT
1. Top utility header
   Brand, tagline, search, mode toggle, and a compact local entry point.

2. Lead news band
   One large lead story card for the most important story on the web right now.
   This card should show:
   - headline
   - 2 to 3 sentence summary
   - source cluster line
   - updated time
   - category
   - button to open the Live News story page
   - button to read the original source

3. Top Stories module
   Keep 8 stories, but stop presenting them as plain list rows.
   Use 2-column desktop cards and 1-column mobile cards.
   Each card should show:
   - headline
   - 1 short summary block
   - source line
   - category
   - published or updated time
   - quality or confidence chip

4. Latest News Feed module
   Keep the grouped-by-time structure, but move to denser cards with clearer text rhythm.
   Each card should show:
   - headline
   - one-line summary
   - source
   - category
   - timestamp

5. Local News module near the top
   Keep this high on the page.
   Keep city launcher behavior and dedicated local page behavior.
   Do not bury local under national or business modules.

6. Category lanes
   Introduce lighter modular sections below the main story area:
   - National
   - International
   - Business
   - Tech
   - Sports
   - Entertainment
   Each lane can show 3 to 5 curated cards and a "See more" action.

7. Search and utilities
   Add a visible site search box near the top.
   Add a "Developing / Live / Explained" filter later.
   Add a compact "Updated in the last hour" quick filter later.

PROPOSED ARTICLE CARD FORMAT
For both Top Stories and category modules, use box-based cards instead of stretched rows.

Card anatomy:
- Eyebrow: category plus urgency state
- Headline: 2 to 3 lines max
- Summary: 2 sentences max
- Source line: primary source plus supporting source count
- Time line: published or updated time
- Actions: Open Live News page, Original source

This will make the page feel more editorial and less like a scraped feed.

HOW THE LIVE NEWS ARTICLE SYSTEM SHOULD WORK
The article system should be helpful, but not "too intelligent." It should not invent new
reporting. It should behave like a structured summarizer and organizer.

SAFE WRITING MODEL
The system should write:
- a clean headline in Live News wording
- a 2 to 4 sentence summary
- 3 key points
- a "why it matters" line
- a source block

The system should not write:
- long opinion pieces
- speculative claims
- quotes that are not present in source material
- dramatic or sensational language
- full rewritten versions of a publisher article

RECOMMENDED ARTICLE PAGE STRUCTURE
1. Live News headline
2. One-deck summary under the headline
3. Story facts section with 3 to 5 bullets
4. Why it matters section
5. Source list with original publisher links
6. Related coverage section
7. Social package or clip promo area later
8. Last updated timestamp

EXTRA INPUTS THAT WILL MAKE LIVE NEWS BETTER
- topic tags for recurring subjects
- live update flag
- correction flag
- explainer flag
- data-source confidence score
- duplicate cluster score
- source freshness score
- manual editor notes
- clip package status
- SEO keyword theme
- internal related-story IDs

EDITORIAL GUARDRAILS
Live News should remain source-respectful and useful. The system should follow these rules:
1. Use source material to understand facts, not to mimic the publisher's prose.
2. Keep summaries short and original.
3. Show where information came from.
4. Prefer multiple-source clusters when possible.
5. Mark uncertain or developing stories clearly.
6. Preserve timestamps so users understand freshness.
7. Let human edits override auto-generated copy.

GOOGLE DOCS COLLABORATION WORKFLOW
The easiest collaboration method is not to build a full CMS first. Start with a simple
editorial loop using Google Docs.

Recommended Google Drive structure:
- Live News / Editorial / Story Drafts
- Live News / Editorial / Approved Stories
- Live News / Editorial / Style Guide
- Live News / Editorial / Source Notes

For each story draft, use a standard document template:
1. Story ID and category
2. Original source link
3. Additional source links
4. Live News headline draft
5. Summary draft
6. Key points
7. Why it matters
8. Notes to Create Clips
9. Status: Draft / Review / Approved / Published

The website should eventually support:
- "Create draft doc" action
- "Mark reviewed" action
- "Publish to Live News page" action

But version 1 can be manual:
1. Auto-generate a story packet.
2. Paste it into a Google Doc.
3. Edit with collaborators.
4. Approve the final summary.
5. Publish the edited fields back into Live News.

EXECUTION PHASES
PHASE 1: DATA MODEL AND STORY PACKETS
Goal: turn feed items into structured Live News story records.
Steps:
1. Expand the backend story object beyond title, link, and summary.
2. Generate a stable slug and story ID for each cluster.
3. Preserve original source links and source counts.
4. Add summaryShort, summaryLong, and keyPoints fields.
5. Store article-ready metadata for SEO and internal routing.

PHASE 2: DEDICATED LIVE NEWS STORY PAGES
Goal: each important story gets its own Live News URL.
Steps:
1. Create a story route, such as /story/:slug
2. Render Live News copy, not raw publisher copy
3. Show the original source link prominently
4. Add structured data for NewsArticle
5. Add canonical tags and related stories

PHASE 3: HOMEPAGE REDESIGN
Goal: make the front page easier to scan and more editorial.
Steps:
1. Convert Top Stories rows into box cards
2. Add a lead story area above the Top Stories grid
3. Keep Local News near the top
4. Keep Latest News grouped by time
5. Rebalance spacing, card density, and summary lengths

PHASE 4: NAVIGATION AND DISCOVERY
Goal: help users find what matters quickly.
Steps:
1. Add search near the top navigation
2. Add section quick links
3. Add filters for live, local, and recent later
4. Add related story linking
5. Add topic tags or recurring themes later

PHASE 5: SEO AND TRUST
Goal: make Live News indexable, understandable, and credible.
Steps:
1. Add per-story title and description fields
2. Add NewsArticle schema where appropriate
3. Maintain sitemap coverage for article pages
4. Show updated timestamps clearly
5. Add About, Contact, and editorial standards pages

PHASE 6: EDITORIAL WORKFLOW
Goal: keep the writing simple, accurate, and collaborative.
Steps:
1. Create a draft template for Google Docs
2. Add human review status to story packets
3. Let approved text override automated summaries
4. Build a later admin tool only after the workflow is proven

PHASE 7: CREATE CLIPS INTEGRATION
Goal: use Create Clips as the attention engine, not the source of truth.
Steps:
1. Send approved Live News article packets to Create Clips
2. Receive social caption and visual packages
3. Link social posts back to the Live News story page
4. Keep attribution and source links intact

HIGH-PRIORITY CLEANUP ITEMS FROM THE CURRENT SITE
1. Remove the user-facing 170 button from the feed controls if 170 is meant to remain a
   backend cap only.
2. Replace long Top Stories rows with structured cards.
3. Add clearer summary text to more homepage story surfaces.
4. Build true Live News story destinations instead of linking headline clicks only to the
   original publisher.
5. Add search and improved section discovery.

SUCCESS METRICS
Live News is moving in the right direction if:
- more users open Live News story pages before leaving to original sources
- article pages receive index coverage in Google
- homepage click-through improves
- local page engagement stays strong
- summaries remain readable and do not trigger copying concerns
- Create Clips social posts send traffic back to Live News pages

BOTTOM LINE
Live News should evolve from a feed-led site into a story-led site. The right move is not
manual article writing. The right move is a structured article generation system with
human collaboration, stronger cards, better front-page hierarchy, and dedicated Live News
story pages that Create Clips can promote.
