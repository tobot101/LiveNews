LIVE NEWS SUMMARY DRAFT
Former Philippine president Rodrigo Duterte is set to face trial on crimes against
humanity charges, according to cross-source reporting collected by Live News. Coverage
clustered by Live News shows PBS NewsHour, ABC News, The Guardian, and Fox News all
reporting the same major development. Current reporting says Duterte, who served from
2016 to 2022, was arrested in the Philippines last year and flown to The Hague. He denies
the charges.

KEY POINTS
1. Multiple major outlets are reporting that Duterte will face trial on crimes against
   humanity charges.
2. The clustered coverage says he was arrested in the Philippines last year and taken to
   The Hague.
3. Duterte denies the charges.

WHY IT MATTERS
This is a major international accountability story involving a former head of state. It
is also the kind of story where cross-source confirmation is important, because readers
need context and clarity rather than a single-source headline.

ATTRIBUTION LINE
Sources: PBS NewsHour, ABC News, The Guardian, and Fox News via Live News clustering.

EDITOR NOTES
- Keep wording neutral and factual.
