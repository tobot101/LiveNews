1. Product Direction To Preserve

Live News is readability-first, low-clutter, source-respectful, and built to move people from social platforms to exact Live News article pages. The social app should support that direction, not become a separate public product inside the website.

  • Brand remains Live News with the tagline Anytime & Anywhere.
  • No ads are added to the website experience.
  • No community hub is being built into Live News.
  • Every public story and social post must respect attribution and original source links.
  • Social posts should use Live News wording and should not copy publisher wording or public comments.
  • Posts should point to exact internal story pages, not the homepage.
  • Human approval stays required until the system has a proven quality record.

4. Publishing Flow

  1. Live News collects and organizes source-linked news items.
  2. Article agents prepare review-only story drafts.
  3. The editor approves a story inside the private Story Approval dashboard.
  4. Approval creates a public /stories/... page with source attribution.
  5. Social Publisher refreshes private Instagram and Facebook draft packages.
  6. The editor reviews caption, title framing, hashtags, image URL, and teacher checks.
  7. The Facebook API button posts only if the exact article URL is available and Meta readiness passes.
  8. After posting, aggregate performance can be recorded in Performance Memory.

5. Safety Layers

The app is intentionally built like a protected system instead of a one-click auto-poster. It publishes only after the story page and social package pass multiple gates.

  • Exact Link Gate: social posts must use exact Live News story pages, not the homepage.
  • Human Approval Gate: drafts stay private and review-only before publishing.
  • Source Safety Teacher: checks attribution and original source links.
  • Human Relevance Teacher: checks that captions have a real reader angle.
  • Rhythm Variety Teacher: blocks repeated robotic openings and repeated structures.
  • Platform Fit Teacher: checks platform-specific caption and media needs.
  • Growth Memory Teacher: learns from aggregate results, not private data.
  • Meta Publishing Guard: blocks posting when variables, permissions, or tokens are not ready.

6. Most Recent Quality Upgrade

After the first successful API post, the weakness was not the API. The weakness was the caption quality. The social writing engine has now been upgraded so the API publishes stronger material.

  • Facebook captions no longer default to stiff Top Story: title wording.
  • Captions now lead with one useful, human-readable detail when source-safe data exists.
  • Posts include a clearer exact-article call to action: Read the Live News page:.
  • Hashtags are smaller and more relevant, such as #LiveNews, category tags, and topic tags.
  • Internal workflow wording is blocked from public captions, including review-only and source packet.
  • Audience pattern scoring was fixed so unrelated patterns do not attach just because they are high priority.
  • Approved stories now pass richer context into social drafts: dek, summary, key points, why-it-matters, image fields, and original publisher title.

8. Growth Memory Rules

The app should get smarter over time by learning from aggregate signals. It should not become a scraper of people, private messages, random public comments, or copied creator language.

Allowed learning Blocked learning
Reach, views, likes, comments count, shares, saves, link clicks, profile visits, follows, hides, reports, posting time, category, caption shape, media shape, and exact article clicks. Private messages, personal profiles, individual identities, private contact details, real access tokens, copied comment text, and unsupported claims from comments.
Aggregate comment patterns such as confusion, useful questions, correction requests, or tone problems. Copying public comments into captions or treating comments as verified facts.
Public platform best-practice patterns such as clear hooks, relevant hashtags, and original source-linked wording. Cheap engagement bait, outrage bait, unrelated hashtags, misleading captions, or copied publisher structures.
